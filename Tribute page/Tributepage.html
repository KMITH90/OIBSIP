Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, fondly remembered as the "Missile Man of India," was a visionary scientist, inspirational teacher, and the 11th President of India. Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to become a key figure in India's space and missile development programs, playing a pivotal role in the 1998 Pokhran nuclear tests. As President, he won the hearts of millions, especially the youth, with his humility, wisdom, and dedication. Dr. Kalam was also a prolific author, known for books like Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds, and remained a passionate advocate for education and innovation until his last breath on 27 July 2015.
